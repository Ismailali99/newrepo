Steve "The Crocodile Hunter" Irwin

  • 1962 - Born in Victoria, Australia
  • 1970 - Moved to Queensland where his parents opened "The Queensland Reptile and Fauna Park"
  • 1971 - Wrestled his first crocodile at the age of 8
  • 1979 - Steve became a crocodile trapper as well as graduating highschool
  • 1992 - Steve took over his parents wildlife park and renamed it the Australia Zoo and marries Terrie Raines at the age of 29
  • 1996 - Steve Irwin's documentary series "The Crocodile Hunter" hits the air becoming Animal Planet's highest rated series
  • 1998 - "The Ten Deadliest Snakes in the World" airs for the first time as well as the birth of Steve's first child, Bindi Irwin
  • 2001 - Awarded the Centenary medal for service to global conservation and Australian tourism
  • 2002 - Wildlife Warriors: originally called the Steve Irwin Conservation Foundation, the Wildlife Warriors was established
    to educate people in the protection of injured, threatened and endangered wildlife. Steve also stars in the hit movie "The crocodile Hunter: Collision Course"
  • 2003 - Steve's second child Robert Irwin is born
  • 2006 - Steve was fatally stabbed in the chest by a stingray barb while filming a documentary "Ocean's Deadliest" at the age of 44
